Karim looked at the face of the person in front of him, there was a foreboding smile on that face, yet Karim was not able to look away from him. The man’s face was a piece of art; it perplexingly blended the charm of a teenage prince and the alure of a mystical siren. The voice that called out to Karim gave Karim a sense of dread, yet it mesmerized him into not walking away. It felt as if all the nectar of the world tried to tie down the bee named Karim to the rotating mahogany chair with red antique leather cushioned around it.

Karim was flabbergasted by the weight of the question. He did not know what was going on and what sort of situation he was in. The sheer fact that he was the sole customer of the Bar during hours when places like this are supposed to be crowded baffled him. He was also concerned about the fact that there seemed to be no one around there other than Mahi. The general ambience and silence of the place sent chills down Karim’s spine.

Karim, regaining a bit of his conscious thinking questioned Mahi in a trembling voice, “What second chance are you talking about? And can you please tell me for God’s sake, what sort of place this is and where are the other customers? “; Karim took a pause seemingly to see the smile in Mahi’s face widen up, he again asked “Who are you and why are you alone here? Please answer me for the love of God!”. Karim’s throat dried up trying to utter the last question.

Mahi stood still on the other side of the serving table cleaning a glass; he was calm and had a seemingly-comforting looking smile carved on his beautiful face. He looked at his watch once and then again started cleaning another glass. He answered in his same gleeful tone, “For God’s sake you say sir? Did you commit all the acts that made your life like this for his sake? No right? You see I was the brightest Child among my brothers; everyone envied me including the parent that had birthed me. When I tried to live my life the way I wanted along with some of my brothers who loved me, my parent and a few brothers conspired against me and banished us from our own home. The God that you speak of didn’t help me then. As to what I am, I am a good Samaritan who cannot keep silent when he sees someone in need. As to what this place is, quite obviously it’s a bar and I have named it the Fidi Abode”.

Karim, having heard Mahi’s story, was able to relate to the pain he might have suffered. Karim was a victim of parental abuse as well; he was constantly beaten up by his father and had fled from his home during his college days to escape the abuse. He worked odd jobs and paid for his own education until the day he had gotten a white-collar job. He asked Mahi out of empathy, “Do you still feel bad about that time? Has life treated you the same way as it has treated me?”.

Mahi answered again in his calm, composed tone “Yes sir! I can feel the same pain in you that I had to face. That’s why I want to reach out my helping hand to you, to help you get a second chance. My father never gave me a second chance; if I do the same, I would be like him. Thus, I like to provide to the people abandoned by life a small helping hand that would allow them to pick up themselves from the mess they are. “

Mahi passed onto Karim, the glass of Martini he had been making; he said “Don’t worry sir, it’s on the house!” and this time without hesitation Karim held onto the glass and took a small sip. Karim asked Mahi, “But you’re just a bartender, how would you be able to help me! I’m a prodigal man who had been consumed by the same poison that I’m consuming now, after my wife and kids left me. How can you save a wretched unworthy man who had been abandoned by his own family he wished to protect ?”.
Mahi looked up to the dull light of the chandelier and replied, “I once helped a couple who had been blinded by unfruitful happiness, realize the truth of the world. I am someone who helps others just for the sake of it. Believe me when I say that I can help you out. But for now, our time together must have come to an end as I feel that I have to return back home now. It’s a place far far away from here.”

Karim replied, “So should I come back tomorrow and you can tell me how you’re going to turn around my life with a second chance ?”

Mahi, looked at his watch back with a shrewd serenity wrapped around his face. He started speaking again to Karim, “No, not tomorrow. I don’t have my place open all the time. I only open it when I have a hunch that someone would be visiting me today and would need my help. You’re in luck actually as I celebrate my birthday on the 30th of April. Come back to this place on that day and I’ll have the contract that allows you have a chance at life again. Now sir, please enjoy your drink while I wrap up the establishment for today.”

Karim sipped away the glass of Martini and looked around himself once more thinking about the absurdity of the situation. But something that happened here had magically cheered him up. He got up from the chair and walked towards the small doorway to the bar. While exiting the place Karim asked Mahi, “What is it there in for you for helping a man like me ?”

Mahi turned his face mechanically at Karim and answered, “What I want weighs nothing, yet it is the heaviest thing you carry around!”. After saying that he started cleaning the martini glass Karim had just used. Karim shut the door behind him after hearing the answer, he was in too much of an internal turmoil to answer a puzzle like that. He told himself that he would return back on Mahi’s birthday to this very place. Even if it seemed implausible , Karim wanted to hold onto the last shimmer of hope he could get.
